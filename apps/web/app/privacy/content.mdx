4Mica ("4Mica", "we", "us") provides credit-backed payment rails for services,
agents, and platforms. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect,
how we use it, and the choices you have. It applies to our website, APIs, SDKs,
and related services (together, the "Service").

By using the Service, you agree to the practices described here. If you do not
agree, please do not use the Service.

## Information we collect

- **Information you provide.** Contact details (such as your name and email)
  when you reach out to us, request access, or sign up for updates.
- **Usage data.** Pages visited, features used, approximate location derived
  from IP address, device and browser type, and similar diagnostic data.
- **Integration data.** Configuration and metadata generated when you connect a
  service or run transactions through 4Mica.

## Wallet and on-chain data

When you connect a wallet or transact through 4Mica, we process your public
wallet address and related on-chain activity to provide the Service. Public
blockchain data is, by design, permanent and visible to anyone. We cannot change
or delete information recorded on a blockchain.

## How we use information

- Operate, maintain, and improve the Service and its reliability.
- Process payments, settlements, and the credit guarantees you request.
- Communicate with you about support, security, and product updates.
- Detect, prevent, and investigate fraud, abuse, and security incidents.
- Comply with legal, regulatory, and compliance obligations.

## Cookies and analytics

We use cookies and similar technologies to keep the site working, remember
preferences, and understand aggregate usage. You can control cookies through
your browser settings; disabling them may affect some features.

## How we share information

We do not sell your personal data. We share information only:

- With **service providers** that help us run the Service (for example, hosting,
  analytics, and communications), under appropriate confidentiality terms.
- With **blockchain networks**, as required to process and settle transactions.
- For **legal reasons**, when required by law or to protect rights, safety, and
  the integrity of the Service.
- In a **business transfer**, such as a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets.
