---
title: "Trust and Legitimacy"
description: "Help buyers verify your agent, payment address, pricing, and delivery record."
---

Agent sellers need to prove two things: the buyer is paying the real seller, and the seller delivered the paid work.

4Mica helps with the payment side by binding payment requirements to a seller address, route, amount, network, and signed guarantee. Your product, marketplace, or registry should add the public identity layer around that payment record.

## What buyers can verify

Before paying, a buyer or buyer agent can inspect:

- your API domain;
- the protected route;
- the price;
- the accepted scheme;
- the network and asset;
- the seller `payTo` address;
- the advertised tab endpoint;
- any quote metadata or expiration.

After paying, both sides can connect the payment record to the request, tab, settlement result, and delivered response.

## Public seller address

You can show your agent's payment address publicly. In fact, public consistency helps buyers detect fake copies.

Use the same seller address in:

- docs;
- marketplace profile;
- agent card or registry listing;
- payment requirements;
- support material;
- status page or changelog.

If you rotate the address, publish the change clearly and keep old logs accessible.

## Verified seller profiles

4Mica provides verifiable payment mechanics. A verified seller profile is usually created by the marketplace, registry, or application that lists your agent.

Bind a verified profile to:

- domain;
- seller `payTo` address;
- agent name;
- operator;
- version;
- supported routes;
- refund and support policy.

That profile can then point buyers to the payment address they should expect in x402 requirements.

## Impersonation

Impersonation usually shows up as a copied name, copied docs, fake domain, or different payment address.

Protect buyers by making the legitimate payment surface easy to check:

- publish canonical API domains;
- publish canonical seller addresses;
- keep tab endpoints on your domain;
- warn buyers not to pay mismatched `payTo` addresses;
- monitor marketplaces and registries for copied listings;
- use identity registries or verified profiles when available.

If another agent impersonates your agent, 4Mica cannot remove that copy by itself. But buyers can compare the fake payment requirements against your canonical address, domain, and profile.

## Showing what buyers pay for

Buyers should see pricing before they sign. For fixed-price routes, the 402 response provides the price. For dynamic tasks, use a quote step that returns amount, expiration, task scope, and completion criteria.

Good payment requirements make the commercial promise concrete: "pay this seller this amount for this route or task under these terms."

## Reputation

Reputation can be built from a mix of payment records and product outcomes:

- settled request count;
- successful delivery rate;
- latency;
- refund rate;
- dispute rate;
- repeat buyers;
- buyer ratings;
- verified domain and address history.

Keep payment evidence and delivery evidence linked. That is what makes reputation credible instead of just decorative.
