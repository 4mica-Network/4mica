---
title: "Facilitator URLs"
description: "Understand when you use the facilitator and why buyers should use your tab endpoint."
---

You use the facilitator from server-side middleware. Buyers usually should not call a hosted facilitator URL directly. They call your API, receive HTTP 402, and use the tab endpoint you advertised in the payment requirements.

That distinction matters because you own the commercial context: route, price, accepted networks, accepted assets, `payTo` address, logging, and abuse policy.

## URLs your service uses

| URL type | Who calls it | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Protected route | Buyer or buyer agent | Requests the paid resource. |
| Your tab endpoint | Buyer or buyer agent | Opens or reuses a tab for your route. |
| Facilitator API | Your middleware | Verifies and settles signed payment guarantees. |
| Core4Mica contracts | Payer or seller through protocol operations | Collateral, settlement, claim, and withdrawal enforcement. |

## Why advertise your own tab endpoint

Your tab endpoint lets you keep your policy at the edge of your own API. It gives you a place to:

- bind the tab to the route and price the buyer saw;
- apply allowlists, blocklists, and rate limits;
- attach request metadata for support and audit;
- hide infrastructure details from buyers;
- migrate facilitator deployments without changing your public payment surface.

In production, the advertised endpoint should be an HTTPS URL on your domain, such as:

```txt
https://api.example.com/payment/tab
```

If buyers are seeing a facilitator URL instead of your tab endpoint, review seller endpoint configuration in [4mica dashboard](https://app.4mica.io).

## Hosted facilitator

Hosted deployments commonly use:

```txt
https://x402.4mica.xyz/
```

Use hosted or self-hosted facilitator URLs from your server configuration. Do not make buyers depend on those URLs directly unless you are intentionally exposing a lower-level integration.

## Operational guidance

- Keep the tab endpoint stable even if the facilitator URL changes.
- Log every tab open request with route, buyer, network, price, and response.
- Reject tab opens for routes, prices, assets, or networks you no longer accept.
- Treat unexpected direct facilitator usage as an integration smell unless you explicitly documented it.
