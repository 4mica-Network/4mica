---
title: "Risk and Abuse Prevention"
description: "Protect paid agent routes from spam, free-trial abuse, failed payments, and suspicious buyers."
---

Agent traffic is fast. A small mistake in pricing, pre-work, or free trials can become expensive quickly.

The seller rule is simple: do not spend meaningful resources until payment validation or another explicit trust decision has happened.

## Spam requests

Unpaid requests should be cheap. Your API can return HTTP 402 without running the expensive model, tool call, search, or workflow.

Add controls around the unpaid edge:

- rate limit unpaid requests;
- cache payment requirements for common routes;
- reject oversized request bodies before quote generation;
- require a quote step for expensive work;
- monitor repeated 402s without paid retries.

## Free-trial abuse

Free trials are not payment guarantees. Treat them as marketing spend with strict limits:

- cap trial value per wallet, account, IP, agent identity, or API key;
- expire credits quickly;
- require a funded payment path before high-cost trials;
- block repeated trial farming patterns;
- convert unknown agents to small paid trials when abuse appears.

## Buyer failure claims

When a buyer claims your agent failed, check three records:

1. payment record: did the buyer sign and did settlement succeed?
2. delivery record: did your system return the promised response?
3. product rule: did the result meet your published completion criteria?

If the issue is a seller-side technical failure, refund or credit according to policy. If the issue is subjective output quality, use the terms you published for that route.

## Spend and request limits

Limit exposure per buyer or agent:

- maximum requests per minute;
- maximum paid value per hour;
- maximum concurrent tasks;
- maximum downstream spend;
- maximum task duration;
- maximum unpaid quote attempts.

You can enforce limits by payer address, agent identity, account, domain, API key, tab, or IP depending on what your integration captures.

## Suspicious activity

Pause or reject payment flows when you see:

- repeated invalid signatures;
- payment headers reused across routes or prices;
- mismatched `payTo`, amount, network, or asset;
- sudden traffic spikes from a new payer;
- repeated failures after delivery;
- patterns that farm trials or refunds;
- requests that would cost more to fulfill than the payment covers.

Payment validation is necessary, but not the whole abuse system. Keep application-level policy close to the paid route.

## Deposits, collateral, and buyer confidence

4Mica's credit model is backed by payer collateral. Sellers should still choose when extra deposits or prepayment are appropriate:

- expensive long-running jobs;
- custom work with subjective acceptance;
- workflows with high downstream cost;
- unknown buyers with no reputation;
- tasks that cannot be cheaply interrupted.

For small deterministic requests, a valid payment guarantee may be enough.

## Risk checklist

- Verify and settle before paid work.
- Rate limit unpaid and invalid requests.
- Cap buyer spend and concurrency.
- Keep free trials narrow.
- Log every paid request and response.
- Define refund rules before launch.
- Monitor claim windows.
- Block suspicious payers quickly.
