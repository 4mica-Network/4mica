---
title: "Trust and Verification"
description: "Help buyer agents decide which sellers, agents, routes, and payment requirements are safe to pay."
---

Before a buyer agent spends money, it should know who it is paying and why that seller is allowed.

4Mica verifies payment mechanics. Your buyer application should verify the commercial context: seller identity, domain, route, price, reputation, and permissions.

## What buyers should verify

Before signing a payment, check:

- seller domain;
- seller `payTo` address;
- advertised tab endpoint;
- route and method;
- price and accepted asset;
- network;
- seller profile or registry entry if available;
- agent or service reputation if available;
- task match.

If the seller address, tab endpoint, or price does not match the service the agent intended to call, do not sign.

## Seller verification

A verified seller profile is usually provided by a marketplace, registry, or application. Bind that profile to the values the buyer sees in payment requirements:

- agent name;
- operator;
- domain;
- `payTo` address;
- supported routes;
- version;
- refund or support policy.

4Mica can give you a payment record. A profile gives you the human-readable identity layer around that record.

## Reputation and history

Buyers may want to see:

- number of successful paid requests;
- repeat buyers;
- latency;
- refund rate;
- dispute rate;
- ratings and reviews;
- examples of outputs;
- version history.

Treat reputation as a decision input, not a replacement for policy. A reputable seller can still be too expensive for the current task.

## Fake or malicious agents

A fake seller may copy an agent name or description while using a different domain or payment address. Buyer agents should compare the payment requirement against a trusted source.

Block or require approval when:

- the `payTo` address is new for a known seller;
- the tab endpoint is not on the seller domain;
- the price changed unexpectedly;
- the seller asks for a network or asset you do not normally use;
- the route does not match the task.

## Revocation

Buyers should be able to stop future spending by:

- disabling the agent;
- changing policy;
- removing allowed sellers;
- revoking or rotating signing keys;
- withdrawing available collateral after checking open tabs and obligations.

Revocation should be planned before production. It is much easier to stop one scoped agent key than to recover from a shared key with broad authority.
