# Error Handling

Buyer errors usually fall into four buckets: unsupported network, missing collateral, invalid signature, or seller-side verification failure.

## Buyer Responsibilities

- Maintain a funded collateral position on the networks you want to use.
- Register a 4Mica client scheme for every network your target sellers accept.
- Sign only payment requirements that match your agent policy.
- Track open tabs and settlement obligations before withdrawing collateral.

## Recommended Flow

1. A payer deposits ETH or a supported ERC-20 into the Core4Mica vault.
2. A seller protects an HTTP route with x402 payment requirements and advertises a tab endpoint.
3. The buyer receives HTTP 402, opens or reuses a payment tab, signs a guarantee, and retries the request.
4. The seller verifies and settles the payment through the facilitator, then serves the protected response.
5. The payer settles the tab after the credit window, or the seller claims from collateral during the on-chain claim window if the payer defaults.

## Operational Notes

Use small prices and testnet collateral first. Once the flow is stable, add spend limits per task, allowlists for seller domains, alerts for failed settlements, and a withdrawal process that checks open tabs before moving funds.
