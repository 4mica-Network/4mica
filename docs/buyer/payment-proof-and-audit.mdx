---
title: "Payment Proof and Audit"
description: "See what your agent paid for, why it paid, and how payments connect to task results."
---

The buyer should be able to audit the full workflow after a task is done.

4Mica gives the payment layer a record of signed guarantees, tabs, settlement, and claim paths. Your application should connect those records to task logs, reasons, outputs, receipts, and support workflows.

## What to record

For every paid request, log:

- task ID;
- request ID;
- agent ID or wallet;
- seller domain;
- seller `payTo` address;
- route;
- amount;
- network and asset;
- tab ID;
- policy version;
- reason for payment;
- approval record if one was required;
- response status;
- output hash or delivery marker;
- settlement or obligation status.

## Why each payment happened

A payment without a reason is hard to trust. Buyer agents should attach a short reason:

- "weather API needed for travel plan";
- "model reranker needed to compare search results";
- "translation agent needed to process seller page";
- "paid dataset required for final answer";
- "compute service needed for image generation."

Reason codes make support, auditing, and user trust much easier.

## Receipts and invoices

4Mica provides the payment mechanics and records. Your application can expose receipts or invoices using those records:

- task summary;
- seller;
- amount;
- date;
- network and asset;
- request ID;
- tab ID;
- result status;
- refund or dispute status.

For business users, export these records in the format their accounting or compliance process needs.

## Disputes

If a buyer disputes a payment, review:

1. Did the agent sign under policy?
2. Did the seller receive a valid payment guarantee?
3. Did the seller return the promised response?
4. Did the output meet the seller's published completion rule?
5. Does the seller refund policy apply?

4Mica helps establish payment facts. Quality disputes, refunds, and support decisions depend on seller terms and application policy.

## Notifications

Notify users when:

- a payment needs approval;
- a task budget is nearly exhausted;
- a payment fails;
- a seller is blocked;
- a large payment is made;
- a task finishes with a spend summary;
- a dispute or refund status changes.

Notifications turn invisible agent spending into visible delegated work.
