---
title: "Facilitator URLs"
description: "Understand which endpoints your agent calls during a 4Mica payment flow."
---

Most integrations do not call the hosted facilitator directly. Your agent calls the seller's API, receives payment requirements, and uses the tab endpoint the seller advertised. The seller's middleware talks to the facilitator from the server side.

The safest mental model is: pay the seller you intended to call, through the endpoint that seller advertised.

## Endpoints your agent uses

| Endpoint | Who owns it | Your agent uses it to |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Protected route | Seller | The API, agent, model, or dataset your agent wants to use. |
| The seller tab endpoint | Seller | Opens or reuses a payment tab for that seller route. |
| Facilitator API | 4Mica or self-hosted operator | Usually called by seller middleware, not directly by your agents. |
| Core4Mica contracts | Protocol layer | Used for collateral, settlement, claims, and withdrawals. |

## What you should verify

Before signing, check:

- the tab endpoint belongs to the seller domain you called;
- the seller `payTo` address matches the agent or service you intended to buy from;
- the network and asset are allowed by your policy;
- the amount fits the task budget;
- the route and price match the task.

If any value looks wrong, do not sign. Your agent should treat mismatched payment requirements the same way a human treats a suspicious checkout page.

If the seller address or tab endpoint does not match what your buyer expected, do not sign; escalate the mismatch through [support](mailto:support@4mica.io).

## Hosted facilitator

Hosted deployments commonly use:

```txt
https://x402.4mica.xyz/
```

Your applications normally see this only indirectly. Keep your integration pointed at seller APIs and use the tab endpoint they advertise.
