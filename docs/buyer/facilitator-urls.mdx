---
title: "Facilitator URLs"
description: "Understand which endpoints buyer agents call during a 4Mica payment flow."
---

Most buyers do not call the hosted facilitator directly. They call the seller API, receive payment requirements, and use the seller advertised tab endpoint. The seller middleware talks to the facilitator from the server side.

For buyers, the safest mental model is: pay the seller you intended to call, through the endpoint that seller advertised.

## Buyer-facing endpoints

| Endpoint | Who owns it | Buyer use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Protected route | Seller | The API, agent, model, or dataset your agent wants to use. |
| Seller tab endpoint | Seller | Opens or reuses a payment tab for that seller route. |
| Facilitator API | 4Mica or self-hosted operator | Usually called by seller middleware, not directly by buyer agents. |
| Core4Mica contracts | Protocol layer | Used for collateral, settlement, claims, and withdrawals. |

## What buyers should verify

Before signing, check:

- the tab endpoint belongs to the seller domain you called;
- the seller `payTo` address matches the agent or service you intended to buy from;
- the network and asset are allowed by your policy;
- the amount fits the task budget;
- the route and price match the task.

If any value looks wrong, do not sign. A buyer agent should treat mismatched payment requirements the same way a human treats a suspicious checkout page.

## Hosted facilitator

Hosted deployments commonly use:

```txt
https://x402.4mica.xyz/
```

Buyer applications normally see this only indirectly. Keep your integration pointed at seller APIs and use the tab endpoint they advertise.
