4Mica is the clearing house for the agentic economy: a credit layer for x402 that lets agents and applications transact instantly, earn yield on collateral, and settle net positions later.

If you are new to agentic commerce, start with the Background pages first. They explain why autonomous agents need machine-readable payments, portable identity, stablecoin settlement, and explicit trust boundaries. This section starts where that background ends: how 4Mica turns those needs into a working payment flow.

Instead of forcing every request to become an on-chain transaction, 4Mica lets a payer deposit collateral once, sign off-chain payment guarantees per request, and settle later. Resource servers keep the familiar HTTP 402 flow while gaining programmable credit, non-custodial enforcement, and batched settlement.

## Who 4Mica is for

- **API providers** that want to charge per request without accounts, invoices, or prepaid balances.
- **AI agents** that need to pay many services during a task and settle once later.
- **Applications** that want x402-compatible payments with credit, collateral, and net settlement.
- **Protocol teams** building financial infrastructure for high-frequency agent-to-agent commerce.

## The basic story

1. A payer deposits ETH or a supported ERC-20 into the Core4Mica vault.
2. A seller protects an HTTP route with x402 payment requirements and advertises a tab endpoint.
3. The buyer receives HTTP 402, opens or reuses a payment tab, signs a guarantee, and retries the request.
4. The seller verifies and settles the payment through the facilitator, then serves the protected response.
5. The payer settles the tab after the credit window, or the seller claims from collateral during the on-chain claim window if the payer defaults.
